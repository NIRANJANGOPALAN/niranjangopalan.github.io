Summary
This research proposes a transformative approach for Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) to
revolutionise support for levy payers through strategic technological integration. By leveraging
digital platforms, and intelligent infrastructure, BIDs can create dynamic, responsive business
environments. The proposed model focuses on real-time economic intelligence, personalised
business insights, and adaptive support mechanisms that enable businesses to navigate
complex market landscapes more eectively.
What is a BID:
A Business Improvement District (BID) is a dened geographical area where businesses
collaborate to enhance their local environment and economy. The key feature of a BID is a levy
charged on eligible business rate payers, supplementing their standard business rates. This
additional funding is strategically invested in projects and services designed to benet the local
business community. The scope of BID initiatives is broad and exible, limited only by the
requirement that they must provide services beyond those already oered by local authorities.
Common areas of focus include:
1. Enhanced safety and security measures
2. Improved cleansing and maintenance
3. Environmental enhancements
4. Marketing and promotional activities
5. Business support services
Traditionally, BIDs operated within single local authority boundaries. However, a signicant
policy change in April 2013 introduced Cross Boundary Business Improvement Districts. This
innovation allows BIDs to extend their operations across multiple local authority jurisdictions,
facilitating more comprehensive and potentially impactful initiatives. The BID model empowers
businesses to take a proactive role in shaping their commercial environment. By pooling
resources and collaborating on shared objectives, BIDs can implement targeted improvements
that might otherwise be beyond the scope of individual businesses or local government
capabilities. This approach fosters a sense of community among businesses while driving
economic growth and area revitalization.
How BIDs work:
Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) have become a popular way for UK businesses to team
up and spruce up their local areas. Imagine a group of shop owners, restaurateurs, and oice
managers all pitching in to make their street or neighborhood more attractive and successful.
That's essentially what a BID does.
Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) have become a signicant tool for local economic
development in the UK since their introduction in the early 2000s. BIDs are dened areas where
businesses collaborate to enhance their local environment and economy through an additional
levy on business rates (Cotterill et al., 2019).These BIDs rst popped up in the UK around the
early 2000s, thanks to some new laws that made them possible. It's like the government gave
businesses a green light to form their own little improvement clubs. Scotland jumped on the
3
bandwagon a few years later, seeing BIDs as a great way to breathe new life into town and city
centers.
The concept of BIDs was rst introduced in the UK through the Local Government Act 2003, with
detailed regulations following in 2004 for England and Wales (UK Government, no date).
Scotland adopted BID legislation in 2007, primarily as a regeneration tool for town and city
centers (Cotterill et al., 2019). As of 2023, there are hundreds of these BIDs scattered across the
British Isles, pumping millions of pounds into local economies. It's quite impressive when you
think about it over a hundred thousand businesses working together to make their areas
better! There are 335 BIDs in the British Isles, contributing over 150 million annually to the
economy and representing over 118,000 businesses (Association of Town and City Management
et al., 2023). BIDs typically operate for a 5-year term before requiring re-approval through a
ballot process (Sandford, 2018).
Here's how it works: businesses in a BID area agree to pay a bit extra on top of their usual
business rates. This extra money goes into a pot that's used to fund all sorts of improvements. It
could be anything from hiring extra security guards to keep the streets safe, to organizing fun
events that bring in more customers, or even sprucing up the look of the area with new plants
and decorations.
Every ve years, the businesses get to vote on whether they want to keep the BID going. It's like a
mini election, where they decide if the extra cost is worth the benets they're seeing. This voting
system ensures that BIDs stay focused on what the local businesses want and need.
The UK government seems to be a fan of BIDs too. They've been checking in on how well they're
working and even oering loans to help new BIDs get started. It's like they're giving a thumbs up
to businesses taking charge of improving their own neighborhoods.
BIDs have also shown their worth during tough times. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, many
BIDs stepped up to support local businesses and communities, proving they're not just about
making things look pretty, but also about pulling together when they get tough. (Association of
Town and City Management et al., 2023).
The UK government has shown continued support for BIDs, including a review in 2014 and a
consultation in 2015 to further improve their eectiveness (Sandford, 2018). Additionally, a BID
Loan Fund was established to facilitate the creation of new BIDs, oering loans between
10,000 and 50,000 to developing BID partnerships (Sandford, 2018).
In essence, BIDs are all about businesses taking the initiative to create better places to work,
shop, and live. It's a grassroots approach to urban improvement that seems to be making a real
dierence across the UK.

Constraints in BIDs:
Limited empirical evidence on long-term economic impacts. (Grail, J. et al, 2020)
Research on BIDs' long-term eects is still evolving.
Studies show mixed results on residential property values.
In New York City, BIDs had lasting eects on commercial property values (16%
increase) but not on residential properties. (Furman Center, 2020)
Concerns about nancial transparency and accountability. (Grail, J. et al, 2020)
BIDs are not public bodies and are not subject to the same transparency requirements.
93% of BIDs make their accounts publicly available, but only 31% use their websites for
full access to working papers.
Transparency issues have led to local disputes, such as in East Riding Council where
businesses were taken to court for non-payment.
Potential for crime displacement to neighboring areas. (Faggio, G, 2022)
While BIDs eectively reduce local crime (2% decrease per quarter in England and
Wales from 2012-2017), criminal activity may be displaced to nearby areas.
Research noticed crime displacement to commercial areas 1-2 kilometers away from
BIDs.

Harnessing Digital Transformation: How BIDs Can Support Business
Growth
In today's fast-paced world, businesses are rapidly evolving through digital transformation. It is
imperative that we utilize digital platforms eectively, leveraging both technology and human
resources. In the UK, there exists a wealth of talent capable of identifying business needs and
facilitating growth. BIDs can play a pivotal role in harnessing these resources to support local
businesses.
The Role of BIDs in Business Growth
BIDs provide an excellent framework for delivering essential services that can help businesses
modernize and thrive. By adopting innovative strategies, BIDs can assist businesses in several
critical areas:
1. Financial Analysis and Advice: Many businesses struggle with nancial management
and planning. BIDs can oer nancial analysis services that help businesses
understand their nancial health, identify areas for improvement, and develop
strategies for sustainable growth. This could include workshops on budgeting, cash ow
management, and investment strategies tailored to the specic needs of local
enterprises.
2. Digital Media and Marketing Support: In an age where online presence is crucial, BIDs
can facilitate access to digital marketing resources. This includes training sessions on
social media marketing, search engine optimization (SEO), and content creation. By
equipping businesses with the skills to eectively market themselves online, BIDs can
enhance their visibility and customer engagement.
12
3. Continuous Monitoring and Support: BIDs should implement systems for continuous
monitoring of business performance. This could involve regular check-ins with local
businesses to assess their challenges and successes. By providing ongoing support and
motivation, BIDs can help businesses navigate ups and downs, ensuring they remain
resilient in the face of adversity.
4. Operational Enhancements: BIDs can assist businesses in improving their operations
by oering consultancy services that focus on eiciency and eectiveness. This may
include process optimization, technology integration, or customer service training. By
enhancing current operations or services, BIDs can help businesses become more
competitive.
5. Networking Opportunities: Facilitating connections between local businesses is
another vital function of BIDs. By organizing networking events, workshops, and forums,
BIDs can foster collaboration among businesses. This not only encourages the sharing
of best practices but also creates a supportive community where businesses can thrive
together.
Challenges in Implementation
While these initiatives are promising, adapting to modern business needs is not without its
challenges. Implementing these strategies requires commitment from BID management and
active participation from local businesses. Additionally, there may be resistance to change
among some business owners who are accustomed to traditional methods.
Conclusion
In conclusion, Business Improvement Districts have a unique opportunity to modernize their
approach by providing essential services that align with the evolving needs of businesses in a
digital age. By focusing on nancial guidance, digital marketing support, operational
enhancements, and fostering a collaborative community, BIDs can signicantly contribute to
the growth and sustainability of local enterprises in the UK.By leveraging the capabilities of local
talent and embracing innovative solutions, BIDs can ensure that they remain relevant and
eective in supporting the dynamic landscape of modern business.
